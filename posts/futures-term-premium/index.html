Futures term premium

June 3, 2025 · 1 min · 198 words

What are term premiums?#

Term premium is the additional yield investors demand for holding longer-term bonds or securities compared to shorter-term ones, compensating for risks like interest rate fluctuations, inflation uncertainty, and reduced liquidity over extended periods.

How much is the premium?#

Let’s do some very crude calculations. (Don’t try this at home)

Future Name Month Last
M2KM5 JUN 2025 2058.30
M2KU5 SEP 2025 2077.50

Difference = New Value - Old Value = 2077.50 - 2058.30 = 19.20 Percentage Growth = (19.20 / 2058.30) × 100 ≈ 0.932% The expected growth from June 2025 to September 2025 is approximately 0.93%. So the prediction is only 0.9% growth in 3 months. This seems mispriced. Now we need to estimate what growth are we expecting. Let’s assume 8% per year so 8% / 4 = 2% but you can put whichever number you want think is the correct estimation. We don’t want to adjust for inflation here because we also aren’t adjusting for inflation in the futures. https://www.lazyportfolioetf.com/etf/ishares-russell-2000-etf-iwm/ Assuming so we are getting 2% - 0.93% premium, which seems really good.
